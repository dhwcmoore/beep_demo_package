Early Warning System

Beep Before Bang

We monitor a compute cluster from two independent perspectives and raise a warning before structural failure becomes operationally visible.

The problem
Conventional monitoring

Detects failure after it becomes visible — alerts fire when services are already degraded, traffic is already rerouting, and the window for clean intervention has already closed.

Beep Before Bang

Detects structural breakdown as it begins — before the partition is complete, before the alert queue fills up, while there is still time to isolate, reroute, or recover cleanly.

How it works
Signal 1 — Temporal
Rupture engine
Detects regime shifts in cluster load telemetry using change-point analysis. Fires when behaviour departs from the established baseline.
+
Signal 2 — Structural
Invariant engine
Monitors the cluster's topology via algebraically-certified Betti numbers. Fires when connectivity departs from the certified design state.
→
Output
Fused early warning
Composite risk score with actionable advisories — reroute, isolate, or halt cross-island workloads — before full partition is visible.

Why it matters
The system remained quiet during the full calm phase. The warning candidate was raised on the last day of calm and confirmed within two periods of stress onset — before the partition was operationally visible, while rerouting and workload isolation were still clean options.

Earlier warning means a larger response window. The difference between a controlled failover and an uncontrolled outage is often measured in minutes.
